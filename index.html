Research Study

Factors Hindering Implementation of Security Measures on Online E-Commerce Systems in Meru County

Analyzing the intersection of technological barriers, economic constraints, and user behavior in Kenya's emerging digital marketplace.

View Dashboard Download Full Report

Abstract

As e-commerce continues to grow in Meru County, the vulnerability of these systems becomes a critical concern. This research explores the significant obstacles that prevent businesses from adopting robust security frameworks.

The study identifies a multi-faceted challenge including high costs of implementation, lack of technical expertise among SME owners, and a general lack of awareness regarding cybersecurity threats.

Research Methodology

A rigorous approach was taken to ensure data accuracy and representative findings across Meru County.

Target Population

E-commerce platform owners, IT administrators, and online consumers within Meru County's major urban centers.

Data Collection

Mixed-method approach utilizing structured questionnaires, semi-structured interviews, and direct system observations.

Analysis Tools

Quantitative data analyzed via SPSS, while qualitative insights were coded using thematic analysis techniques.

Strategic Recommendations

1

Technical Training

Subsidized workshops for SME owners to build foundational cybersecurity skills and system maintenance knowledge.

2

Policy Enforcement

The Meru County government should implement local digital trade policies that mandate basic security audits for e-commerce licenses.

3

Cloud-Based Solutions

Encouraging the transition to reputable cloud hosting providers that offer built-in security features at competitive rates.

4

User Education

Continuous awareness campaigns for consumers on the importance of strong passwords and recognizing phishing attempts.
